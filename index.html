About Me

Hey! I'm Shabi. I am an open-source enthusiast, security advocate, and computer scientist.

I'm interested in most things computer and technology related. My favorite topics within computer science are theoretical computer science (e.g. computational complexity theory) and cryptography, while I also care deeply about privacy. I work as a researcher in the field of verifiable cryptography.

Aside from computers, I enjoy going on walks, video games, and chess.

Research

I am a PhD researcher at Airbus in collaboration with the Hamburg University of Technology (TUHH), working at the Institute for Cyber-Physical Systems. I hold a Bachelor's and Master's degree in Computer Science from the Karlsruhe Institute of Technology (KIT).

My focus is verifiable cryptography for aerospace systems. I explore how zk-SNARKs, zk-STARKs, and related proof systems can be made suitable for safety-critical aerospace environments. Besides Verifiable Computation, I am broadly interested in cryptographic primitives with a prover-verifier setting.
